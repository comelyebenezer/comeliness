Federal Government Agency

Timeline

Role

Full-Stack Development

Users

The Challenge

The government needed a unified digital platform to replace fragmented legacy systems. Citizens faced long wait times, redundant paperwork, and inconsistent service delivery across 12 federal agencies. The existing infrastructure couldn't handle peak traffic loads, leading to frequent downtime during critical periods.

Our Approach

We designed a microservices-based architecture with a single sign-on (SSO) system that unifies access across all government services. The platform features a citizen portal, an agency dashboard, and a robust API layer that connects to existing legacy systems without requiring full replacement.

Key Features

The platform includes document management with OCR capabilities, real-time status tracking for applications, multi-language support, offline-first mobile experience for rural areas, and automated notification system via SMS, email, and push notifications.

The Result

The platform transformed how citizens interact with government services. Application processing time dropped from 30 days to under 48 hours. The system handles 500K+ daily transactions with zero downtime since launch. The project was recognized by the UN as a model for digital government transformation in developing nations.
