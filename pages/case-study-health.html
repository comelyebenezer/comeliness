Client

National Health Ministry

Timeline

20 Months

Role

System Architecture

Records

500K+ Patients

The Challenge

The national healthcare system relied on paper-based records across 200+ hospitals and clinics. Patient data was fragmented, leading to duplicated tests, medication errors, and inability to track disease outbreaks in real-time. The system needed to be HIPAA-compliant, accessible in remote areas, and handle massive data volumes.

Our Approach

We designed a centralized Electronic Health Records (EHR) system using Angular for the frontend, .NET for the backend, and SQL Server for the database. The system features role-based access control, audit logging, and encryption at rest and in transit. We built a mobile app for healthcare workers in areas with limited connectivity.

Key Features

The platform includes patient registration and demographics, medical history tracking, prescription management with drug interaction alerts, lab results integration, appointment scheduling, telemedicine capabilities, and real-time epidemiological dashboards for disease surveillance.

The Result

The system unified patient records across 200+ healthcare facilities, enabling seamless care coordination. Medication errors dropped by 70%, average patient wait time decreased by 45%, and the real-time surveillance system detected a disease outbreak 3 days faster than the previous paper-based reporting system.
